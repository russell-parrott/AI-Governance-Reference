# Institutional Roles

**[→ View this section on the AI Governance Reference](https://russell-parrott.github.io/institutional-roles.html)**

This section describes the professional actors and institutions involved when automated systems are overseen, investigated or enforced against.

Entries explain the roles that appear when AI governance is tested in practice: regulators, data protection authorities, auditors, compliance officers, legal counsel, board members, investigators and courts. Each entry explains what the role involves, what it can examine and what it requires from the organisations it scrutinises.

Understanding who conducts oversight — and what they look for — is inseparable from understanding what governance must demonstrate. The institutional landscape shapes what evidence matters and what accountability means in practice.

The tests these institutions apply are set out in [Governance Questions](https://russell-parrott.github.io/governance-questions.html). The failures they most commonly find are described in [Governance Failures](https://russell-parrott.github.io/governance-failures.html).
