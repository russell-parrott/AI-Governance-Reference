# Jurisdictions

**[→ View this section on the AI Governance Reference](https://russell-parrott.github.io/jurisdictions.html)**

This section explains how AI governance obligations appear in different countries and legal systems.

Entries cover 13 jurisdictions, examining how each approaches automated decision-making accountability, algorithmic transparency, data protection and AI-specific regulation. The section allows direct comparison of how the same governance questions — who is responsible, what records must exist, what oversight is required — are answered in different legal environments.

AI systems operate across borders. The legal obligations they create do not disappear because a system was built elsewhere or a decision was made by an algorithm rather than a person.

The underlying legal concepts are explained in [Law & Regulation](https://russell-parrott.github.io/law-regulation.html). The broader legal frameworks those jurisdictions apply are covered in [Legal Landscape](https://russell-parrott.github.io/legal-landscape.html).
