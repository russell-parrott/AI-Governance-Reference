# Records & Evidence

**[→ View this section on the AI Governance Reference](https://russell-parrott.github.io/records-evidence.html)**

This section explains what information must be recorded and preserved to explain automated decisions.

Entries describe the documentation, logs, decision records and audit trails that allow an organisation to reconstruct what a system did, when it did it and on what basis. Concepts include audit trail, decision logging, data retention, evidential reconstruction and record integrity.

Governance is what can be demonstrated. Investigation, litigation and regulatory scrutiny all begin with the same question: what records exist? This section explains what adequate records require and what their absence means when decisions are challenged.

This section does not address who is responsible for maintaining records — that is covered in [Authority & Responsibility](https://russell-parrott.github.io/authority-responsibility.html). The legal obligations that make records mandatory are covered in [Law & Regulation](https://russell-parrott.github.io/law-regulation.html).
