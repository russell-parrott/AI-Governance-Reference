# Governance Questions

**[→ View this section on the AI Governance Reference](https://russell-parrott.github.io/governance-questions.html)**

This section sets out five practical tests for assessing whether an organisation demonstrably controls its automated systems.

The five questions are: What is the system? Who is responsible for it? How is it controlled? What records exist? What legal obligations apply? Each test identifies what evidence governance actually requires — not what a policy document claims, but what an organisation can demonstrate when examined.

These tests are used by regulators, auditors and investigators to determine whether governance exists in practice or only on paper. They are the difference between claimed accountability and demonstrated accountability.

Common ways organisations fail these tests are examined in [Governance Failures](https://russell-parrott.github.io/governance-failures.html). The substantive concepts behind each question are explained throughout the core sections of the reference.
