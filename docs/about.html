About AI Governance Reference

Terms such as accountability, audit trails, human oversight, intended use and regulatory investigation appear regularly in board discussions, regulatory guidance, technical documentation and media reporting. These terms are often used as if their meaning is obvious. In practice, they are frequently misunderstood.

AI Governance Reference exists to close that gap.

The AI Governance Reference is a structured collection of short reference briefings, each explaining a single governance concept in clear, non-technical language. Every briefing stands on its own, so a reader can look up a single term and understand what it means without needing to read the rest of the collection. At the same time, each briefing is connected to related concepts, allowing the reader to follow the structure of governance as it operates in practice.

The material is organised across 10 areas that together describe how automated decisions are governed and examined.

The AI Governance Reference Structure

  • System Properties (SP) — what automated systems are and how they behave
  • Authority & Responsibility (AR) — who is accountable for automated systems
  • Control Systems (CS) — how systems are monitored and controlled
  • Records & Evidence (RE) — what must be recorded and preserved
  • Law & Regulation (LR) — the legal obligations automated systems create
Supporting reference sections:
  • Legal Landscape (LL) — the legal frameworks automated systems trigger
  • Jurisdictions (JX) — how governance obligations appear in different countries
  • Governance Questions (GQ) — five tests for whether governance exists
  • Governance Failures (GF) — structural governance breakdowns regulators find
  • Institutional Roles (MK) — the professional actors involved in governance
Together these elements reflect what scrutiny actually looks like. When automated decisions are questioned, regulators, investigators and courts do not ask whether the technology was impressive. They ask whether the system was governed properly, whether responsibility was clear, and whether the organisation can demonstrate what happened.

AI Governance Reference explains the concepts that answer those questions.

The AI Governance Reference is designed for anyone who needs to understand governance language quickly and accurately. Directors preparing for board discussions, managers overseeing automated systems, lawyers examining regulatory exposure, journalists reporting on enforcement actions and students studying technology governance can all use it as a reference.

It is not a compliance checklist, a technical manual or legal advice. It is a reference tool that explains what the key governance concepts mean and how they connect.

In short, AI Governance Reference is a map of the concepts that define accountability for automated decisions.
