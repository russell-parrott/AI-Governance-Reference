Authority & Responsibility

When an automated system makes a decision that affects someone, the question that follows is always the same: who was responsible? The answer depends on whether accountability was genuinely assigned, whether roles were clearly defined, and whether the people named as responsible actually had the authority and information to exercise that responsibility. In practice, these conditions are often assumed rather than established.

Authority and Responsibility explains the concepts that determine whether accountability is real or nominal, accountability, role definition, delegated authority, board and management responsibility, vendor accountability, and the gaps that appear when responsibility is unclear. These are the concepts regulators examine and courts apply. Organisations that cannot answer the basic accountability questions before something goes wrong will find them very difficult to answer after.
