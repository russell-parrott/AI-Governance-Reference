# GF-07 — System Scope Expansion Without Approval

> **A system is used for purposes beyond those originally authorised.**

---

## What This Failure Is

The system was approved for a specific use. It is now being used for something else — a different population, a different decision type, a different geography, a different operational context. The expansion happened incrementally, through operational convenience rather than governance decision. No one formally decided to expand the scope. No one formally assessed whether the original governance arrangements covered the new use.

The system is operating outside its authorisation. The governance that was established for the original use does not govern the expanded use. The organisation may not know this is happening.

---

## Where It Occurs

Scope expansion without approval occurs when teams discover additional uses for an available system and adopt them without governance review; when systems are extended to new markets or geographies without re-assessment of regulatory obligations; when efficiency pressures create incentives to use existing approved tools for new purposes; and when governance frameworks focus on initial deployment approval without creating a mechanism to review scope changes.

---

## Why It Matters

The governance that authorised the system — the risk assessment, the legal compliance analysis, the accountability structure, the oversight arrangements — was specific to the original use. The expanded use has not been governed. It may carry different regulatory obligations, different population characteristics, different risk profiles and different accountability requirements. The organisation is operating an ungoverned system in the expanded context while believing it is operating a governed one.

---

## How It Is Discovered

Scope expansion is discovered during operational reviews that compare actual use against authorised scope; during regulatory investigation that examines whether current use matches governance documentation; during litigation, when the specific use being challenged turns out to postdate the governance assessment; and during audit, when monitoring of what the system is doing reveals uses not covered by the governance framework.

---

## Evidence That Reveals It

System logs showing use cases not covered by the deployment authorisation; absence of governance records for the expanded use; deployment documentation describing a narrower scope than current operations; and regulatory mapping that does not cover the obligations triggered by the expanded context.

---

## Governance Concepts Involved

→ SP-09 (Intended Use) — the authorised scope the expansion has exceeded
→ SP-10 (Unintended Use) — the expanded use now occurring
→ SP-30 (Deployment Authorisation) — the record that defines authorised scope
→ CS-10 (Change Management) — the process that should have governed the expansion
→ SP-24 (Deployment Context) — the changed context that requires its own governance

---

## What Organisations Often Assume Instead

That approval for one use implicitly covers related uses. That the system working in the new context means it is appropriately governed for that context. That governance is about the system rather than about the combination of system and deployment context.
