# GF-06 — Vendor Reliance Without Governance

> **An organisation assumes vendor assurances substitute for internal governance.**

---

## What This Failure Is

The organisation deploys a vendor-supplied AI system. The vendor provides documentation, certifications and contractual assurances about the system's performance, compliance and governance. The organisation treats those assurances as governance. It conducts no independent assessment. It maintains no internal governance records. When something goes wrong, it points to the vendor.

The vendor's documentation describes the vendor's governance of the system. It does not describe the deploying organisation's governance of the deployment. The organisation that deployed the system is accountable to regulators and affected individuals for how it was used. That accountability does not transfer through a contract.

---

## Where It Occurs

Vendor reliance without governance occurs most commonly when systems are procured from established vendors whose brand creates organisational confidence; when procurement decisions substitute for governance decisions; when AI systems are classified as standard software procurement rather than as governance-significant deployments; and when the organisation lacks the technical capacity to conduct independent assessment and defaults to vendor assurance instead.

---

## Why It Matters

Deploying organisations are accountable for the decisions made by the systems they deploy — including vendor systems. A regulator investigating harm caused by a vendor AI system will examine the deploying organisation's governance, not the vendor's. The deploying organisation cannot point to the vendor's documentation as its governance position. Its governance position is what it did — and if the answer is that it relied on the vendor's assurances and did nothing further, that is a governance failure.

---

## How It Is Discovered

This failure is discovered when investigators examine the deploying organisation's internal governance records and find only vendor documentation — no independent risk assessment, no deployment authorisation based on internal review, no monitoring independent of vendor reporting, and no governance records that the organisation itself produced.

---

## Evidence That Reveals It

Governance files composed entirely of vendor documentation; absence of internal risk assessment records; absence of deployment authorisation records based on internal governance review; monitoring records that are vendor reports rather than independent organisational monitoring; and the inability of the organisation to answer basic governance questions about the system without referring to vendor documentation.

---

## Governance Concepts Involved

→ AR-08 (Vendor Responsibility) — the boundary this failure misunderstands
→ AR-09 (Procurement Governance) — the process that should have created internal governance
→ SP-30 (Deployment Authorisation) — the internal governance decision that was not made
→ CS-01 (Monitoring) — the ongoing governance the organisation substituted vendor reporting for
→ RE-13 (System Documentation) — the internal documentation the organisation failed to produce

---

## What Organisations Often Assume Instead

That vendor contracts transfer accountability to vendors. That vendor certifications constitute organisational compliance. That procuring a compliant product means the deployment is compliant. That the vendor's governance is the organisation's governance.
