# JX-00 — Jurisdiction Series

> **AI governance concepts are universal. The legal obligations that enforce them are not. This series maps the regulatory environment that organisations face in thirteen major jurisdictions.**

---

## What This Series Is

The Jurisdiction Series explains the AI governance and regulatory landscape in specific countries and regions. Where the Legal Landscape series (LL-00 through LL-11) explains what frameworks apply globally, this series explains what they look like in practice in each jurisdiction — which regulators enforce them, what specific laws are in force, and what obligations organisations face on the ground.

Each card covers one jurisdiction. It describes the current regulatory framework, names the bodies with enforcement authority, explains what triggers governance and legal obligations, and connects those obligations to the concepts defined in the Atlas.

---

## Why Jurisdiction Matters

The governance concepts in the Atlas apply everywhere. Accountability is accountability whether a system operates in Seoul or São Paulo. But the specific legal obligations that flow from those concepts — what must be documented, who must be notified, what rights individuals hold, what regulators can demand — vary significantly by jurisdiction.

An organisation deploying automated systems across multiple markets faces a layered obligation: the universal governance requirements that good practice demands, and the specific legal requirements that each jurisdiction imposes. This series addresses the second layer.
