# GF-15 — Control Without Documentation

> **Governance actions occur but are not recorded in a way that can later be demonstrated.**

---

## What This Failure Is

Governance happened. Reviews were conducted. Concerns were raised. Decisions were made. Oversight was exercised. Nobody wrote it down — or what was written down is insufficient to reconstruct what occurred. The governance was real at the time. It is not demonstrable now.

This failure is the inverse of records created after the event (→ GF-09). There, records are fabricated to replace governance that never happened. Here, governance occurred but was not recorded in a form that survives scrutiny. The outcome is the same: the organisation cannot demonstrate what it did.

---

## Where It Occurs

Control without documentation occurs in governance cultures where the emphasis is on doing rather than recording; in fast-moving operational environments where documentation is treated as overhead; in informal governance processes where verbal decisions and email exchanges substitute for formal records; and in organisations where governance processes were designed to function but not to produce evidence of their functioning.

---

## Why It Matters

Governance that cannot be demonstrated is, for accountability purposes, governance that did not occur. The standard applied by regulators, courts and auditors is not whether the organisation exercised good judgment but whether it can demonstrate that it did. An organisation that exercised genuine oversight but has no record of doing so is in the same evidentiary position as one that did not.

This failure matters most in the context of investigations and litigation, where the burden of demonstrating adequate governance falls on the organisation — and the evidence required to meet that burden must come from contemporaneous records.

---

## How It Is Discovered

Control without documentation is discovered when investigators request evidence of governance controls the organisation claims to have operated, and the organisation cannot produce records that demonstrate they functioned. It is confirmed when staff describe governance processes that produced no documentary output — meetings without minutes, reviews without records, approvals without documentation.

---

## Evidence That Reveals It

Absence of records for governance processes the organisation claims occurred; governance processes described by staff that produced no contemporaneous documentation; approval decisions recorded only in emails or verbal accounts; oversight activities with no output record; and incident responses reconstructed from memory rather than documented contemporaneously.

---

## What Organisations Often Assume Instead

That doing the right thing is sufficient without documenting that it was done. That informal governance processes are adequate if the people involved understood their obligations. That evidence of governance will be available when needed without having been created as governance occurred.
