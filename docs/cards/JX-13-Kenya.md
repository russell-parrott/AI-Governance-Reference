# JX-13 — Kenya

> **Kenya has moved faster than most African nations on AI governance — with a National AI Strategy, a Draft AI Code of Practice, and data protection law in active enforcement.**

---

## The Framework

Kenya's AI governance framework combines data protection law with a forward-looking national AI strategy and draft AI-specific governance instruments. The Data Protection Act 2019 is in full force and is actively enforced by the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC). It establishes individual rights, organisational obligations and an enforcement regime that is among the more active on the African continent.

The National AI Strategy 2025–2030, published in 2025, sets out Kenya's ambition to become a regional AI hub and establishes governance principles for AI development and deployment. The strategy addresses accountability, transparency, fairness, human oversight and safety — principles that align closely with the Atlas framework.

A Draft AI Code of Practice is under development and expected to establish voluntary governance standards that will inform future mandatory requirements. Kenya's approach follows a familiar pattern: establish principles, develop guidance, move toward binding rules as the technology and its governance implications become clearer.

The Digital Health Act 2023 addresses AI in healthcare contexts. The Capital Markets Authority and Communications Authority each have regulatory jurisdiction over AI applications in their sectors. Kenya's growing fintech sector — centred on M-Pesa and a vibrant startup ecosystem — means financial services AI is a significant area of practical governance concern.

---

## Key Regulators

**Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC)** — Data Protection Act enforcement, personal data in AI systems, individual rights. The ODPC has issued enforcement notices and fines and has published guidance on data protection and emerging technologies.

**Communications Authority of Kenya (CA)** — telecommunications, digital services, AI in online platforms.

**Capital Markets Authority (CMA Kenya)** — AI in investment management, capital markets, algorithmic trading.

**Central Bank of Kenya (CBK)** — AI in banking and payment services, fintech regulation.

**Competition Authority of Kenya** — consumer protection, digital market conduct, algorithmic pricing concerns.

**Ministry of Information, Communications and the Digital Economy** — national AI strategy, digital governance policy.

---

## What Triggers Obligations

**The Data Protection Act 2019** triggers whenever an AI system processes personal data of individuals in Kenya. Data controllers must register with the ODPC, establish a lawful basis for processing, implement security safeguards, respond to data subject rights requests and maintain processing records. Automated decision-making that significantly affects individuals requires specific justification and individuals have rights to explanation and review.

**The National AI Strategy** triggers governance expectations for organisations operating AI systems in Kenya. While the strategy is not itself binding law, it establishes the principles against which regulatory decisions and future legislation will be assessed. Organisations whose AI systems demonstrably conflict with the strategy's principles face increasing regulatory risk as the framework develops.

**Sector regulation** triggers in financial services (CBK oversight), capital markets (CMA Kenya), and healthcare (Digital Health Act). The CBK has been active in regulating fintech and digital financial services, and AI systems in lending, payments and insurance face regulatory scrutiny.

**Consumer protection law** — the Consumer Protection Act 2012 — triggers for AI in consumer-facing commercial contexts. Misleading AI interactions, AI-driven pricing that disadvantages consumers, and deceptive AI marketing are actionable.

## Governance Connection

Kenya's governance environment is developing rapidly. Organisations investing in AI governance now are positioned both for current compliance requirements and for the more demanding framework that the Draft AI Code of Practice and future legislation will introduce.

**Accountability** (→ AR-01, LL-09) is a Data Protection Act requirement. Data controllers must be registered, identifiable and capable of demonstrating their compliance. The ODPC has conducted compliance audits and enforcement actions against organisations that could not demonstrate adequate governance.

**Transparency** (→ SP-15, LL-02) is required under the Data Protection Act. Individuals must be informed about the processing of their personal data and the logic of automated decisions that affect them. Kenya's National AI Strategy specifically identifies transparency as a governance principle.

**Human oversight** (→ AR-03, CS-13) is addressed in the National AI Strategy and expected in sector-specific regulatory guidance. The CBK's fintech oversight has specifically addressed the governance of automated credit decision systems, including human review obligations.

**Records** (→ RE-07, RE-13) are required under Data Protection Act accountability obligations. The ODPC has demanded records in enforcement investigations. Organisations that cannot produce processing records and governance documentation face enforcement consequences.

**Fairness** (→ SP-19, LL-03) has particular significance in Kenya's context. AI systems deployed in credit, employment and public services affect a population with significant socioeconomic diversity. The National AI Strategy specifically identifies fairness and non-discrimination as governance imperatives, and the Constitution of Kenya's equality provisions provide a constitutional backstop.
