# GF-04 — Policy Without Practice

> **Governance policies exist but are not followed operationally.**

---

## What This Failure Is

The organisation has a governance framework. It has policies covering AI risk, data protection, accountability, monitoring and human oversight. Those policies are documented, approved and available. They are not followed. The operational reality — how systems are actually deployed, monitored and reviewed — does not match what the policy says the organisation does.

This is one of the most common governance failures and one of the most damaging when exposed. The organisation cannot claim it was unaware of its obligations. It documented them. It simply did not meet them.

---

## Where It Occurs

Policy without practice appears wherever governance frameworks are designed for regulatory purposes rather than operational implementation; where policies are drafted by central functions without input from the teams who must follow them; where there is no mechanism to verify whether policies are being followed; and where governance reviews examine whether policies exist rather than whether they are practised.

---

## Why It Matters

When an organisation produces its governance framework during an investigation, regulators and courts examine whether the framework was followed, not just whether it existed. A policy that describes governance controls the organisation did not actually operate is evidence of a more serious failure than having no policy — because it demonstrates the organisation knew what it should do and chose not to do it.

The gap between policy and practice is also the gap between apparent compliance and real accountability. Apparent compliance satisfies a governance checkbox. Real accountability requires that the governance described was governance that functioned.

---

## How It Is Discovered

Policy without practice is discovered when operational records are examined against the governance framework they are supposed to reflect. Investigators compare what the policy requires — documented approvals, regular reviews, oversight records, incident escalation — with what the operational record shows. Where the operational record does not contain the evidence the policy requires, the gap is visible.

---

## Evidence That Reveals It

Absence of records that the policy requires to exist; operational processes that do not reflect the policy's stated procedures; staff who are unaware of or unfamiliar with the governance policy; governance reviews that have not been conducted on the schedule the policy specifies; and incident handling that does not follow the policy's escalation requirements.

---

## Governance Concepts Involved

→ AR-11 (Policy vs Practice) — the governance concept that directly names this failure
→ AR-10 (Internal Governance) — the framework within which this failure occurs
→ CS-09 (Operational Reviews) — the control that would detect and close the gap
→ RE-13 (System Documentation) — the documentation whose absence reveals the failure

---

## What Organisations Often Assume Instead

That producing a governance policy satisfies a governance obligation. That documented intent is equivalent to demonstrated practice. That the absence of complaints or incidents means the policy is being followed.
