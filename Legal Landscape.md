# Legal Landscape

**[→ View this section on the AI Governance Reference](https://russell-parrott.github.io/legal-landscape.html)**

This section maps the legal frameworks that automated systems commonly trigger.

Entries explain major legal frameworks relevant to AI governance: data protection and privacy law, anti-discrimination law, financial regulation, product liability, consumer protection and director duties. Each entry explains what the framework covers, why automated systems activate it and how it connects to the governance concepts in the reference.

AI governance does not exist in isolation from law. Organisations deploying automated systems do not get to choose which legal frameworks apply. This section explains the terrain.

This section does not provide legal advice. The specific legal obligations those frameworks create are covered in [Law & Regulation](https://russell-parrott.github.io/law-regulation.html). How they apply in specific countries is covered in [Jurisdictions](https://russell-parrott.github.io/jurisdictions.html).
