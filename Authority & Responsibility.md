# Authority & Responsibility

**[→ View this section on the AI Governance Reference](https://russell-parrott.github.io/authority-responsibility.html)**

This section explains who authorises automated systems and who is responsible for their outcomes.

Entries describe the roles, responsibilities and decision structures that determine who approves a system, who defines its purpose and who answers for its actions when decisions are questioned. Concepts include accountability, delegated authority, board and management responsibility, and role definition.

Automated systems do not remove accountability. When a decision causes harm, regulators, investigators and courts ask the same question: who authorised this system and who was responsible for its operation? This section explains how those questions are answered.

This section focuses on organisational responsibility structures. Technical system behaviour is covered in [System Properties](https://russell-parrott.github.io/system-properties.html). The records used to demonstrate accountability are covered in [Records & Evidence](https://russell-parrott.github.io/records-evidence.html).
