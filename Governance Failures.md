# Governance Failures

**[→ View this section on the AI Governance Reference](https://russell-parrott.github.io/governance-failures.html)**

This section describes the common structural governance breakdowns that regulators and investigators find when examining organisations that use automated systems.

Entries explain recurring failure patterns: the absence of audit trails, accountability gaps between technical and business functions, oversight structures that exist on paper but not in practice, records that cannot support reconstruction of a decision, and control mechanisms that were never tested. These are not edge cases. They are the failures most commonly found when automated decision-making is examined under scrutiny.

The purpose of this section is descriptive, not prescriptive. It explains what failure looks like, not how to avoid it.

The tests used to identify these failures in practice are set out in [Governance Questions](https://russell-parrott.github.io/governance-questions.html). The institutional actors who conduct that examination are described in [Institutional Roles](https://russell-parrott.github.io/institutional-roles.html).
